Tax Extension vs. Tax Return: What’s the Difference?

Understanding the distinction between a tax extension and an actual tax return is pivotal for any taxpayer. A tax extension allows you extra time to file your tax returns; however, it does not grant you an extension on making tax payments. Here’s how they differ: - **Tax Extension:** This provides an additional six months to file your tax return but does not change your payment due date. Filing a tax extension can help avoid late fees and penalties from the IRS. - **Tax Return:** This is the completed document you submit to the IRS that shows your income and tax calculations for the year.
