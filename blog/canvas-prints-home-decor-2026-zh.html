Blank walls are a missed opportunity. Custom canvas prints turn your favorite photos, travel memories, and family moments into wall art that tells your story. Here's how to do it right.

💡 Key Insight The single biggest mistake people make with canvas prints: ordering too small. What looks large on screen looks tiny on a wall. When in doubt, go one size up from what you think you need.

Choosing the Right Canvas Size for Your Wall

Wall size determines canvas size. For a typical bedroom wall (10-12 feet wide), a 24x36 canvas as a centerpiece works well. For living rooms with tall ceilings, 30x40 or larger creates impact. For hallways, vertical 16x24 or 20x30 works well. Gallery walls with multiple smaller prints (8x10 to 16x20) work anywhere.

  • Small rooms: 16x20 or 20x24 as focal point
  • Living rooms: 24x36 or larger for impact
  • Hallways: vertical format 16x24 or 20x30
  • Gallery walls: mix of 8x10, 11x14, and 16x20
  • Above sofa: two-thirds the sofa width is the rule
  • Gallery wrap eliminates need for framing

Creating a Gallery Wall with Canvas Prints

Gallery walls work best with a mix of sizes — one or two larger pieces (20x24 or 24x30) anchoring smaller prints (8x10 and 11x14). Stick to a consistent color palette or theme. Plan your layout on the floor before hanging. Leave 2-3 inches between prints for breathing room.

常见问题

What size canvas should I get for above a sofa?+
The standard rule is two-thirds the width of the sofa. For a 90-inch sofa, a 60-inch wide canvas or gallery arrangement works well.
What type of photo looks best on canvas?+
Well-lit, high-resolution photos with meaningful subjects. Landscape photos with depth, portraits with emotion, and travel photos with strong composition all work beautifully on canvas.
